Caroline Coolidge, born 1998, is an interdisciplinary artist and writer based in Edinburgh, Scotland. Coolidge's practice sits between painting, printmaking, sculpture, sound, and experimentation. She is interested in technology, the history of science and tools, the unraveling of language, and the construction of reality and meaning. Coolidge received an MFA with Distinction from the Ruskin School of Art at the University of Oxford in 2024 where her work primarily focused on the screen and the multiplicity of meanings associated with this word. Before beginning her master's degree, Coolidge worked as a teaching fellow in the Art, Film, and Visual Studies department at Harvard University, USA, where she completed her BA with Highest Honors in 2022. Coolidge has exhibited her work in solo and group shows and given public lectures at venues including the Dolphin Gallery at St John's College, University of Oxford, UK, the Spoke Gallery, Boston, USA, and the Carpenter Center for the Visual Arts at Harvard University, Cambridge, USA.
